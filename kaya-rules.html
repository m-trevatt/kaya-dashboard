Management Rules

Non-negotiable protocols for anyone handling Kaya

Read this page first if you're looking after Kaya. These rules exist because they work — breaking them undoes weeks of training progress. No exceptions, no shortcuts.

Lead & Handling

Treat before lead removal Always

Every single time. No exceptions. Prevents warning bites.

House line on before pen exit Always

Every time. Control during high-arousal transition.

No sit at back door after toilet Paused

Being rebuilt from scratch separately.

Resource Guarding

Never reach in directly Never

Will trigger warning bite. Use treat scatter — she moves away, then remove object.

Dangerous objects — two-person approach Caution

Treat scatter has ceiling-ed. One person distracts, one removes. Intervene at pickup moment.

Pen & Rest

Treat every time going in pen Always

Apple slice. Positive association.

No toys in pen — ever Never

Pen is for rest. Low stimulation.

Greeting Visitors

20-minute ignore protocol Always

Arms crossed. No dangly items. No eye contact. No talking to her. If over-threshold: remove to garden, re-admit when calm. Showing visible progress — don't skip.

Second Floor Access

Earned privilege Strict

Chew near Juliet balcony for environmental exposure. Single blanket grab, pillow grab, or table lick = immediate removal downstairs. One strike. No warnings. Consistent every time.

Toilet

Toilet is queued, not door-triggered Always

Initiated by owner on schedule, not by Kaya requesting. Prevents demand barking.

Recall

Fish treats are recall-exclusive Always

Only for whistle recall. Never anything else. Keeps association clean and high-value.

Owner Consistency

Both owners follow same rules Priority

Consistency is the central challenge. Inconsistency undoes training fastest. Calm-present-engaged disposition is active practice — nervous system co-regulation.
