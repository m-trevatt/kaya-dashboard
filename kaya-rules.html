Management Rules

Non-negotiable protocols for anyone handling Kaya

Access
FirstAccess comes before everything

She can't guard something she can't reach. Keep shoes, socks, bags, jumpers, towels and similar items out of her reach. Prevention is the whole game, and it stops most guarding before it starts.

Where she can go
UnsupervisedGround floor with the kitchen door and back door closed.
Semi-supervisedFloor 1 with the study door and both bedroom doors closed. Any blanket grab, pillow grab or table lick is an immediate removal downstairs.
Direct supervisionBedrooms, top bathroom, study, summer house. Someone with her at all times.
Check oftenBack yard. Regular checks that she is not licking wee or eating poo. A fox has been up the top lately, let her watch for short intervals and practise whistle recall to avoid prey drive.
No accessShoe cupboard, garden shed, garage.
Resource guarding protocol
NeverDo not take items from her while she is guarding

If she has grabbed and is holding something, do not take it and do not pull the lead. Follow this exactly:

  1. Kaya steals an item.
  2. Close the pen immediately.
  3. She will go to her raised bed.
  4. Do not attempt to take the item from her, no matter what it is. Do not pull on the lead.
  5. Stay calm at all times. Get several high-value treats from the cupboard or fridge.
  6. Walk near her and throw a treat about 1 metre away from her, then walk away.
  7. Return and throw another treat to the other side, further away, then walk away.
  8. Once enough trust is built, get close enough to take the item only when she is far away from you, ideally without her seeing you take it.
  9. The intention is that she senses a human approaching and anticipates something great is about to happen, rather than something being taken away.

If the item gets damaged, we will pay for repairs or replacement. If the item is dangerous, she won't swallow it unless you try to take it directly from her, so do not try.

ResetQuick grab and release

Sometimes she grabs something and lets it go immediately. If she does this, reset her in the pen for 1 to 2 minutes.

Lead & handling
AlwaysTreat before lead removal

Every single time the lead is removed, give a treat before the clip is touched. No exceptions. This prevents warning bites during lead removal, an active problem.

AlwaysHouse line on before pen exit

Attach the house line before Kaya is let out of the pen. Every time. It gives you control while she is excited, coming out of the pen into open space.

Pen & rest
AlwaysTreat every time going in the pen

Kaya gets a treat (apple slice) every time she goes into the pen. Keeps the pen a good thing, every single time.

NeverNo toys in the pen, ever

The pen is for rest. No toys go in. No exceptions. Keeps the pen calm and boring, which is the whole point.

Remove all toys off the ground before you say bed time, to avoid conflict at pen time.

Greeting new people
AlwaysIgnore completely, release from a sit

When someone new enters the room they must ignore her completely, especially keeping hands away from her. Any engagement at all, including saying "no", is read by her as play.

Counter surfing is temporarily allowed during this. Any jump that touches the person is a reset in the pen. Once barking has stopped and she has sat (both required), open the door and give the release command "OK" or "Break". She must wait for the release, or the door closes again. Any additional jumps also get reset.

Manage access to grabbable items (shoes, socks, bags, jumpers) during greetings. There is a real risk of a grab at this moment. If she grabs, switch to the guarding protocol above.

Household manners
NeverNot allowed on furniture

Kaya is not allowed on the furniture. The one exception is when she is invited up to sit on you on the lounge.

AlwaysAll good things come from a sit

Attention, treats, the door opening, a smell of an item, all of it should come from a sit. You shouldn't have to command it, just wait for her to offer the sit.

Toilet
AlwaysButton to queue, prompt before upstairs

Kaya has a button she can push to queue for the toilet. We also recommend prompting her to wee before she goes upstairs.

If she is regularly fussing, meaning taking too long to wee when she goes outside, put the house line on, stand near the pebbles holding it still, and cue "wee-wee" once. If she doesn't wee within 1 minute, cue "ok" and return inside. Repeat as needed.

Recall
AlwaysFish treats are recall-exclusive

The fish treats in the recall bag are only used for whistle recall. Never for anything else. This keeps the whistle recall association clean and high-value.

Hot weather
NeverNo walks at 20°C or above

Samoyeds overheat quickly. At 20°C or above there are no walks. Even in the high teens it is often better to sit on the shaded bench near the Strand instead (check under it for rubbish first). Pavement burns paws, so if it is too hot for your hand it is too hot for her.

See the Routine page for the full hot-weather protocol and cooling activities.

Watch forSigns of overheating

Excessive panting, drooling, bright red tongue or gums, lethargy, stumbling, vomiting. If temperature exceeds 40°C, vet immediately. See the Health page for how to check her temperature.
