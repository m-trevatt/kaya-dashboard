Behaviour Profiles

Arousal, guarding, socialisation, and fear profiles with management strategies

Arousal profile Elevated baseline

Baseline

Higher than normal for her age, confirmed by the trainer. Her first real heat (finished 12 June 2026) has brought it down a little, but it is still high.

Triggers

Greetings with known people Seeing other dogs Lead going on Transitions between activities

Key pattern

Energy mirroring and escalation: If a stimulus reflects energy back at her, she escalates immediately. Calm dog = very excited but manageable. High-energy dog = matches and doubles it. Same with people.

Pre-walk animation is frustration-based, not positive excitement.

Management

We practise engage-disengage outside regularly. The main thing we work on is staying calm ourselves, because she settles when we settle. Copying what the trainer does in person has been the most useful thing by far.

Ball and tug are back since the heat, without the over-excitement they used to cause.

Build trust: Praise her a lot and don't be scared of her. She wants to do the right thing desperately, she just needs to know when she is doing it.
Guarding profile Specific, not generalised

Scope

Specific to self-discovered or stolen objects only. Not generalised - no food bowl guarding, no space guarding.

Indoors

Glasses case, sharp tin lids, screws, bolts - genuinely dangerous items creating genuine dilemma. Current protocol: treat scatter, remove when she moves away, never reach in directly.

Protocol ceiling reached for high-value dangerous items. She's learned the pattern and locks jaw pre-emptively.

Outdoors

Improving. Dynamic movement helping natural drop without direct intervention. Scavenging reduced but ongoing.

Bite incidents

12 June 2026: A dog sitter removed her own cardigan and wrapped it around her waist while walking Kaya. Kaya grabbed the cardigan. The sitter tried to keep hold of it and take it from her, triggering the guarding response. Kaya gave warning bites on the sitter's forearm, breaking skin for the first time.

25 June 2026: A different dog sitter arrived and followed the crate release protocol. Kaya grabbed one of the sitter's trainers. The sitter tried to pull it from her directly rather than using the guarding protocol. Kaya escalated to jumping and grabbing at the sitter's clothing, tearing it, and made contact with the sitter's upper arm. Skin was not broken.

Takeaway: Do not try to take clothing or objects directly from her. Anything that is not life-threatening is left with her, and the guarding protocol on the Rules page is used instead.
Socialisation profile Work in progress

Other dogs

If the other dog is calm, she's mostly well behaved but very excited. This excitement can mask underlying anxiety. If the other dog is high energy, she matches and at least doubles it.

Parallel walking at the enclosed dog field is helping her stay calmer around other dogs. The whistle recall once pulled her off a dog she was reacting to through a fence.

People

Same energy-mirroring pattern. Follow the greeting protocol on the Rules page (ignore completely, hands away, release from a sit). Visible progress.

On-walk management

Cross the road or stick to one side when another dog or person approaches. Sometimes put in a sit, sometimes distraction treats straight on the nose.

Foot-planting: If caught too close to a trigger (someone coming out of an alleyway), she plants her feet and no amount of pulling will move her. Management: ask the person to move on, encourage Kaya to stay on all fours.

After a trigger passes, sometimes follows behind at distance - building proximity without always pulling away. Other times, continues forward without stopping.

Engage-disengage: whenever she is looking, or has been looking, at a trigger, she will come to you for praise. Just say "good girl" and give her pats. If she is locked on to something and won't look away, cue "Yes" and throw a treat the other way.

Untested

Cats, squirrels, and other small animals - no exposure data yet.

Fear profile Minimal

Known responses

Single fear response to a sail being dropped quickly at the front of the house (lives behind a boat yard). Not a problem since.

Fireworks: no issue. Thunder: no issue. Being alone: not really an issue. New environments: gets very excited (arousal, not fear). Handling: generally fine.

Car travel: a bit unsettled initially but settles eventually.
