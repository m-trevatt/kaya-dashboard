Behaviour Profiles

Arousal, guarding, socialisation, and fear profiles with management strategies

Arousal Profile Elevated baseline

Baseline

Elevated beyond typical adolescent norms — confirmed by trainer. First heat produced measurable improvement but still high.

Triggers
  • Greetings with known people
  • Seeing other dogs
  • Lead going on
  • Transitions between activities
Key Pattern

Energy mirroring and escalation. If stimulus reflects energy back, she escalates immediately. Calm dog = very excited but manageable. High-energy dog = matches and doubles. Same with people. Pre-walk animation is frustration-based, not positive excitement.

Management
  • Engage-disengage practiced regularly
  • Owner working on calm-present-engaged disposition
  • Nervous system co-regulation
  • Modelled behaviour from trainer is most effective learning tool
  • Ball and tug reinstated post-heat without problems

Guarding Profile Specific, not generalised

Scope

Self-discovered/stolen objects only. No food bowl guarding, no space guarding.

Indoors

Glasses case, sharp tin lids, screws, bolts — genuinely dangerous items. Protocol: treat scatter, remove when she moves away, never reach in directly. Protocol ceiling reached for high-value dangerous items — she's learned the pattern and locks jaw pre-emptively.

Outdoors

Improving. Dynamic movement helping natural drop. Scavenging reduced but ongoing.

Socialisation Profile Work in progress

Other Dogs

Calm dog = mostly well behaved but very excited (can mask anxiety). High-energy dog = matches and doubles. Parallel walking building calm template. Whistle recall worked for fence-reactive moment.

People

Same energy-mirroring pattern. Greeting protocol in place (20-min ignore). Visible progress.

On-Walk Management

Cross road or stick to one side. Sometimes sit, sometimes distraction treats on nose. Foot-planting when caught too close — she plants feet and won't move. Management: ask person to move on, encourage four-feet stance. After trigger passes, sometimes follows behind at distance.

Engage-disengage: "yes" for looking at trigger, "yes" for looking away, treating at correct moments.

Untested

Cats, squirrels, other small animals.

Fear Profile Minimal

Response History

Single fear response to sail dropping at front of house (boat yard nearby). Not a problem since.

Unafraid Of
  • Fireworks
  • Thunder
  • Being alone
  • New environments (excited/arousal, not fear)
  • Handling
  • Car travel (settles after initial unsettledness)
