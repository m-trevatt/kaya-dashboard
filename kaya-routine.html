Daily Routine

Awake windows, rest, and one wind-down to bed

7:30 – 9:00
Awake & walk
House line only if you are actively training (jumping, grabbing, etc.). Out for a wee first, and prevent licking or eating wee and poo.
Morning walk 30 to 45 minutes, always quit while you are ahead.
Hot weatherAt 20°C or above there is no walk. Do a cooling or find-it game at home instead (see hot-weather protocol below).
9:00 – 10:00
Rest
Pen with treat. Camera monitoring.
10:00 – 14:00
Interaction & play
Sniff games, training, free shaping, find-it, calm play. Paddle bath on hot days (see below).
14:00 – 17:30
Rest
Pen with treat. Camera monitoring.
18:00 – 19:00
Short walk or bench
A short walk, or sit at the shaded bench near the Strand in warm weather (check under it for rubbish first).
19:00 – 21:00
Watch dinner cooking
Watches dinner prep. Minimal interaction. Never interact with her while she is eating.
21:00 – 22:30
Calm
Wind down. Simple known commands only.
22:30
Bed (pen)
Remove all toys off the ground before you say bed time. Last toilet. Pen with treat. Lights out.

Training focus right now

Clothes and object grabbing. Any grabbing is an immediate pen for a minimum of 5 minutes. Multiple times in a row earns a full sleep (2 to 3 hours). Anything not life-threatening is left with her, use the guarding protocol on the Rules page instead.

Hot-weather protocol

Samoyeds run hot. No walks at 20°C or above. Even in the high teens it is often better to sit on the shaded bench near the Strand.

Water: keep lots available. Cycle her water bowls through the fridge and add ice from the freezer.

Cooling: give her the frozen chopping board from the freezer, and freeze wet kibble. A cooling mat is allowed but always supervised. Do not wet her coat on top (underside is fine).

Cool sleep spot: she can sleep in the front toilet instead of the pen, as it stays very cool.

Balcony: allow upstairs access near the Juliet balcony for airflow when it is hot.

See the Health page for how to check her temperature and the signs of overheating.

Hot-day activities

Find it: pen her, hide treats around the whole house, then cue "Find it".

Paddle bath: put the shower head on in the bath facing up, plug down, add her balls, and let her play. Dog towels are in the shoe cupboard on the ground floor.

Foxtails, walking areas

There are currently many foxtails in all the standard walking areas. Avoid them. Her long fur hides them very easily, and you still need to check all four legs when you get home.

Stick to the paved areas around the Strand, or take her the other way through the forest tunnel (expert handlers only).

Rhea takes her semi-regularly to the enclosed dog field for calm parallel walking with other dogs at distance.

Walking setup

No Halti currently. Retractable lead. Heel walk to the park, full 5 metres at the park for sniffing, released with a sit-wait then "break" or "go sniff".

The Strand in mornings, lower footfall, better for threshold management.
