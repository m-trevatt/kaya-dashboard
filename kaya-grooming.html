Grooming

Coat care, desensitisation status, and professional groom notes

Coat

Spring coat blow - active

First winter coat now shedding. Needs regular brushing sessions. Samoyed double coat requires thorough undercoat removal during blow.

Brushing

Generally good with brushing. Has gotten a lot better over time. Regular sessions needed during coat blow.

Watch: Persistent knotting behind right ear. Deep inside the coat. If not tracked across sessions, knots grow large and can't be combed out. May take multiple sessions to clear. Check every brushing session.
Nails

Nail clippers - desensitisation needed

Currently producing warning bites when nail cutting is attempted. This is part of the wider warning bite pattern (see Training & Problems). Needs systematic desensitisation - gradual exposure to clippers with counter-conditioning before any cutting is attempted.

Paws, nose & teeth

Balm - every day

Balm must be applied to her nose and all paw pads every single day. This is a daily job, not an occasional one.

Paw hair trimming

Paw pad fur needs regular trimming. We are actively training her to accept it.

Teeth brushing

In training. Building tolerance for regular tooth brushing.

Washing & drying

Washing

Good with the washing part. Has gotten comfortable with baths.

Blow drying

Not great with blow drying. Was desensitised to it early on but that work has lapsed. Needs reintroduction to prevent regression.

Risk: At a recent professional groom (Eleanor), Kaya didn't manage to fully dry. Incomplete drying can be dangerous for Samoyeds - the dense undercoat retains moisture which can lead to skin problems or hot spots. Blow-dry tolerance needs priority attention.
